Noli Me Tangere Characters

Maria Clara
Maria Clara is the childhood sweetheart and fiance of Noli Me Tangere's main protagonist.
Her name and character has since become a byword in Filipino culture for the traditional, feminine ideal.
Maria Clara symbolizes the purity and innocence of a sheltered native woman during the time of Spanish occupation.
Cristosomo Ibarra
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra is the main protagonist of the novel Noli me Tangere by Jose Rizal.
His full name is Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin, son of Don Rafael.
In the novel, he is frequently referred to as Ibarra.
Padre Damaso
Padre Damaso was a brash and arrogant individual.
Due to the power afforded to him for being a friar, he acted superior to others and was opposed to natives recieving education abroad.
He is a shameless loudmouth, he is unafraid of slandering nonreligious citizens who he thinks undermine his power.
Capitan Tiago
Capitan Tiago was an influential businessman in San Diego and the father of Maria Clara.
Betrothing his daughter to Crisostomo Ibarra, Capitan Tiago struggled to obey the will of the friars
He symbolizes the rich Pilipinos who oppress their fellow Pilipinos in exchange for the influence and the riches that they might gain from their powerful associations.
Dona Victoria
Dona Victorina was a socialite and friend of Capitan Tiago.
She along with her husband Don Tiburcio sought to associate themselves with the influential figures of San Diego in order to gain further social status.
As one of the lesser evils in the novel, Dona Victorina symbolizes those who have a distorted view of their identity.
